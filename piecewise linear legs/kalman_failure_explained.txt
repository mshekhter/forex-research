The core failure is simple:

This is a smoothed derivative classifier with adaptive quantile gates. It is not a robust leg detector.

Its hidden promise is:
“Give me any run, and I will always find some directional bars.”

That is why it still fails after being “optimized.” The optimization is cosmetic. It tunes the noise labeling mechanism instead of changing the objective.

The most damaging line of thought in this design is:
top x% of internal slope z-scores = real legs

That premise is wrong.

What this code does is:
“Partition the price series into locally confident positive, negative, and neutral slope regimes.”

That is not the same as extracting meaningful legs.

The next sensible rewrite is to define legs with an external notion of significance first, 
such as minimum realized displacement, persistence, and tolerance for brief counter-moves, 
and only then use a smoother like Kalman as a support signal rather than as the leg definition itself.
